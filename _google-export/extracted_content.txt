WARA: Wavelet-based Realtime Recovery against Acoustic Injection Attacks on UAV
---
We develop more capable acoustic attacks that simultaneously target multiple heterogeneous IMUs on a drone. Our experiments demonstrate that these attacks, whether remote or touch-based, are effective in disabling the IMU redundancy mechanism on FC boards. The touch-based attack even effectively bypasses the physical shielding on commercial FC boards.
---
We develop WARA, a novel wavelet-based approach to recover benign signals from compromised IMU measurements. WARA separates the benign signal components from the compromised data using wavelet packet decomposition. This design allows WARA to withstand simultaneous acoustic attacks across all the IMU instances, resulting in a more persistent and durable recovery.
---
We implement WARA on the PX4 autopilot and demonstrate that WARA meets the time and resource requirements of real hardware. In the hardware-in-the-loop (HITL) simulation, WARA achieves recovery durations longer than the SOTA PBAR approach, the deep-learning-based denoising approach, classic heuristic filters, and specification-aware recovery.
---
In real-world attack tests, WARA sustained flight for 2 to 5 minutes under attack and landed safely.
---
We adhere to the open science policy. All source code and datasets for both simulations and real-world experiments will be publicly available on Zenodo.
